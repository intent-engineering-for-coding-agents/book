# Foreword

I'll be honest about why I wrote this book.

Open LinkedIn on any given morning and you'll find another carousel: *"The 14 skills you need to master Claude Code."* *"How I built a SaaS in 3 weeks and made $40k."* *"Why every developer must learn AI agents NOW."* Fine. Good for them. But I keep scrolling and waiting for the part that never comes — **how**. How did you actually do it? What did the codebase look like at week two when nothing worked? What did you tell the agent? What did you throw away? What broke in production?

That part is always missing. And I think it's missing on purpose, because the honest answer doesn't fit on a one-pager.

This book is my attempt at the honest answer. It's about using AI **responsibly and professionally** to build real software. Not the kind that demos well on a Tuesday and falls apart on a Friday.

Let me say a few things up front.

**Vibe coding is fine. In its place.** It's excellent for fast prototyping and mockups. It's wonderful when you need to put something on a screen for a customer, get a feel for the shape of a feature, or pull requirements out of someone who can't quite articulate them. I use it all the time for exactly that. But a vibe-coded prototype has no business growing up into a production system. The right move, almost always, is to throw it away and start over with the real requirements in mind. The prototype was the conversation, not the product.

**The AI is not a magic box.** It cannot read your mind. It will not auto-magically produce the code you intended. That is not going to happen. Not today, and not by wishing harder at the prompt box. If you have something in mind, you have to tell it what you have in mind. Spell out the requirements. State the rules, the intention, the tech stack. Use the AI as a sparring partner to sharpen your architecture and tighten your design, but the thinking still starts with you.

**Context is the whole game.** Rather than cramming everything into one-shot prompts you'll have to retype tomorrow, persist your context in documents: plain text and diagrams with real semantics, things both you and the AI can read a month from now. Do this and the quality of the work jumps in a way that feels almost unreasonable. Skip it and you'll be vibe-coding forever, wondering why your agent keeps "forgetting."

## From Vibe to Pro

Here is the part the LinkedIn carousels leave out. If you give your AI enough up-to-date information about the system you intend to build and maintain, you can move past prototypes and produce **production-grade software**. But there is no shortcut around the work software developers have always been expected to do: proper analysis, design, coding, testing, deployment. Exactly what we were taught in school and at university, and exactly what the carousels skip over because it doesn't sell.

Software development is still a craft. Humans must stay in the loop. The AI may catch up to more of this in time, and when it does I'll happily revise this foreword. But for now the responsibility is ours: to be deliberate, to be professional, and to do the part of the job that humans still do best.

That's what the rest of this book is about. The **how**.
