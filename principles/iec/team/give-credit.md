# IEC-TEAM-GIVE-CREDIT: Give Credit Where Credit is Due

**Layer**: 1
**Categories**: team, culture, sourcing
**Applies-to**: all
**Summary**: A book that hides its shoulders is weaker, not stronger — credit sources explicitly.

## Principle

Give credit where credit is due. A book that hides its sources is weaker, not stronger. Every section that draws on a specific reference names it. Every taxonomy or synthesis that is the book's own work is labeled as such. The distinction between primary, secondary, and vendor-authored sources is made visible.

## Why it matters

Citing sources gives the reader the trail back to the evidence. It lets them judge the provenance of the claim. It credits the people whose work the book builds on. And it models the practice for the reader: agentic engineering is built on traceable decisions, not unsourced assertions.

## Violations to detect

- Claims presented as settled without a source
- Book synthesis presented as field consensus
- Missing `*Sources:*` lines at the end of sourced sections
- Sources not appearing in the References section

## Good practice

Every section that makes a factual, historical, operational, or comparative claim carries a `*Sources:*` line. Every reference appears in the plan's References section. Book synthesis is labeled: "This book uses...", "The working taxonomy here is...", "This is a practical synthesis..."
